The Business Problem: The marketing operations division experienced attribution blindness where split, multi-channel advertising budgets masked true conversion paths. Overlapping schedules on distinct networks resulted in significant double-counting of leads, driving up cost-per-acquisition metrics and obscuring real channel return on investment.

The Investigation & Implementation: Granular audience interaction logs were mined and structured through custom normalization parameters inside Python. Relational logic joins inside MySQL successfully decoupled conflicting attribution sequences. The cleaned datasets were connected to interactive reporting workspaces, giving stakeholders clear visual tracking components to refine budget balances and decrease customer acquisition costs.

  • Isolated accurate channel attribution paths from multi-layered clickstreams.
  • Constructed performance tracking matrices across distinct advertising cycles.
  • Supplied interactive visualization frameworks to optimize marketing capital deployment.

The Business Problem: Drastic seasonal spikes and shifting localized buying habits frequently disrupted regional warehouse supply schedules. Peak traffic windows resulted in immediate stock shortages for key store departments, while rapid demand drops caused excessive inventory holding expenses and severe clearance price drop metrics.

The Investigation & Implementation: Millions of operational rows were grouped and structured using optimized query partition rules and windows functions in PostgreSQL. Chronological sequences were extracted to separate underlying seasonal patterns from daily retail noise. Using Power BI Desktop and Advanced Excel dashboard features, a demand intelligence interface was launched to align store inventory updates with historical moving averages, allowing logistics managers to smoothly stabilize shipment frequencies.

  • Queried vast transactional datasets via optimized index parameters.
  • Identified store demand variance values using localized timeline filters.
  • Developed adjustable parameters to establish responsive store fulfillment schedules.
