Marketing-Campaign-Performance-Analytics

Database Engine

Sourced From Case Studies File

The Business Problem: The organization faced severe attribution blindness where fragmented, multi-channel promotional spend masked the actual conversion drivers. Overlapping campaign schedules across distinct advertising channels caused massive double-counting of user interactions, leading to artificially inflated cost-per-acquisition (CPA) parameters and localized ad-budget inefficiencies.

The Investigation & Analytical Implementation: To decouple true campaign profitability, granular customer interaction touchpoints were isolated and transformed through a specialized database structure. By applying schema normalization workflows via Python and script-based transaction aggregation inside MySQL, multi-tier touchpoint pathways were separated successfully.

Cross-platform dashboard systems were then engineered to display live tracking variables, giving management the visualization tools necessary to restructure ad-spend balance ratios and lower acquisition overheads.

  • Decoupled overlapping marketing data loops to isolate true acquisition roots.
  • Uncovered distinct customer engagement groups via SQL-based transaction categorization.
  • Supplied business leaders with interactive canvas layouts to track real-time channel attribution degradation.

Repository Name

Walmart-Retail-Analytics-Demand-Forecasting

Core Technology Stack

PostgreSQL, Python, Power BI Desktop, Advanced Excel

Analytical Methods

Time Series Partitioning, Rolling Drift Analysis, Trend Audit

Database Engine

Structured Multi-Row Storage Engine

Sourced From Case Studies File

The Business Problem: Severe seasonal demand variations and unpredicted localized sales spikes consistently disrupted regional distribution pipelines. High-traffic holiday surges often led to immediate inventory stockouts in high-margin product groups, while sudden traffic drop-offs generated excessive holding expenses and margin decay from forced clearance markdown strategies.

The Investigation & Analytical Implementation: Dense historical sales records were processed using structured window functions and multi-table joins within PostgreSQL. The data was partitioned into discrete chronological sequences to evaluate store-level velocity shifts and isolate seasonal fluctuations from baseline retail noise.

Using Power BI Desktop and Advanced Excel, a demand intelligence portal was created to sync distribution thresholds with moving average trends. This enables warehouse operators to preemptively balance regional stock shipments and limit stock out dependencies.

  • Analyzed multi-row historical transaction indices using optimized relational views.
  • Captured seasonal demand spikes across disparate geographical store locations.
  • Provided distribution teams with parameter tools to adjust warehouse replenishment buffers before major holidays.

Sourced From Case Studies File

The Business Problem: Fragmented regional delivery networks were causing critical fulfillment bottlenecks, dropping customer satisfaction review scores and causing third-party merchants to leave the marketplace. Prolonged shipping channels and inaccurate transit window estimations triggered elevated order abandonment metrics, making spatial logistics tracking essential.

The Investigation & Analytical Implementation: Complex relational queries were deployed to connect geographic master indexes with multi-stage transit tracking variables in a centralized database schema. Order execution paths were broken down into distinct milestone phases to isolate exact delivery delay roots.

Through interactive geospatial map structures inside Tableau and consumer cohort matrices in Power BI, transport bottleneck zones were successfully mapped across regions, providing logistical coordinators with the analytical insights required to audit carrier performance and optimize fulfillment timelines.

  • Isolated transit delay intervals by breaking down individual order lifecycle timestamps.
  • Mapped spatial distribution bottlenecks using interactive coordinate layers in Tableau.
  • Modeled customer purchase cycles using historical cohort retention tracking.
